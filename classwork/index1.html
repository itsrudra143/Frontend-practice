Recipe of Pav bhaji

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (potatoes, carrots, peas, cauliflower), chopped
  • 1 cup bell peppers, chopped
  • 1 cup tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup green peas
  • 4-5 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1-inch ginger, grated
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup pav bhaji masala
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/4 cup butter
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)
  • Lemon wedges (for serving)
  • 8 pav (dinner rolls)
  • Butter for toasting

Instructions:

  1. Boil the mixed vegetables (potatoes, carrots, peas, cauliflower) until they are soft. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large pan, heat oil and butter together.
  3. Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn translucent.
  4. Add minced garlic, grated ginger, and chopped green chilies. Sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
  5. Add chopped bell peppers and tomatoes. Cook until tomatoes are soft and the mixture becomes mushy.
  6. Add pav bhaji masala, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for a couple of minutes.
  7. Add the boiled mixed vegetables and green peas to the pan. Mash the vegetables with a potato masher to get a coarse texture. Mix everything well.
  8. Add a little water to achieve the desired consistency. Simmer for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  9. Slice the pav horizontally. Apply butter on the inside surfaces.
  10. Toast the pav on a griddle or in a pan until they turn golden brown.
  11. Garnish the bhaji with chopped coriander leaves.
  12. Serve hot bhaji with toasted pav, lemon wedges, and additional butter on the side.
  13. Enjoy your homemade Pav Bhaji! You can also serve it with chopped onions and a dollop of butter on top for an extra indulgence.
